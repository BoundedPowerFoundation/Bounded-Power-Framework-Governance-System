# Mechanism Record — Shaker Heights Architectural Board of Review

## SECTION 1 — What is this authority?

**1.1 — Mechanism name:** Shaker Heights Architectural Board of Review (ABR).

**1.2 — Functional description:**
The authority is a three-member appointed board that conducts design review of all new construction and exterior changes to buildings in the City of Shaker Heights. Per Codified Ordinances Chapter 1309, the ABR reviews and must approve plans for: new buildings, additions, window and door changes, auxiliary structures, decks, re-siding, re-roofing, freestanding walls, retaining walls, certain fences, porches, signage, and other exterior alterations. Approval by the ABR is a precondition of building permit issuance by the Department of Building & Housing. The ABR judges projects against Design Guidelines and Standards (most recently revised March 1998 per public-source document), which include specific standards (compliance with which permits administrative staff review without ABR appearance) and general guidelines (used in case-by-case Board review). The ABR also serves as the Board of Building Code Appeals per §1311.01, hearing appeals from Building Code decisions and orders issued by the Director of Building and Housing. In Landmark Districts, per Codified Ordinances §137.12, the ABR's authority is constrained to approve only architectural design compatible with other historical buildings in the district. Routine repair and maintenance that does not produce visual change is not subject to review.

## SECTION 5 — What stops it?

**STOP PATH 1 — Appeal to City Council or Court (per §1309.99 and Ohio law)**
- Who can activate it: any applicant aggrieved by an ABR decision
- Trigger condition: timely filing of administrative appeal to the appropriate appellate body. Specific appeal pathway varies: under Ohio Revised Code §2506, administrative decisions of municipal boards may be appealed to the Court of Common Pleas. UNKNOWN whether Shaker Heights Codified Ordinances provide for an internal appeal to City Council before judicial review.
- What "stopped" means: judicial reversal or modification of the ABR decision; remand for further proceedings
- Independence justification: state courts are structurally independent of the municipality
- Post-stop review process and timeline: judicial timelines per Ohio Rules of Civil Procedure; ORC §2506 sets specific timelines for administrative appeals

ANNOTATION: per public sources, "there is no video for ABR or Board of Appeals meetings" and minutes are posted "a minimum of 30 days" after approval at the next quorum meeting. This produces an asymmetric record availability favoring the Board relative to applicants seeking to build a record for appeal. This is a framework-relevant finding under R44 (Independent Audit Capability) and R81 (Adversarial Exposure Requirement).

**STOP PATH 2 — Shaker Heights City Council**
- Who can activate it: City Council, by ordinance amending Chapter 1309 (composition, authority, procedures, or Design Guidelines), or by Charter action affecting the Board's existence
- Trigger condition: judgment-based, expressed through legislative action
- What "stopped" means: Council can amend ABR scope, modify Design Guidelines (in coordination with the Board), restructure membership, defund staff support, or abolish the Board
- Independence justification: Council is elected by Shaker Heights voters and operates outside the Board's chain of relationship. ANNOTATION: the Mayor appoints ABR members per typical Ohio municipal practice; the Mayor is separately elected. UNKNOWN specific appointment authority for ABR members.
- Post-stop review process and timeline: legislative process on Council's calendar

**STOP PATH 3 — Self-disqualification per §1309.05 and member removal per §1309.02**
- Who can activate it: an ABR member (self-disqualification on matters of self-interest); the appointing authority (member removal under §1309.02; specific grounds UNKNOWN)
- Trigger condition: §1309.05 conflict-of-interest condition; §1309.02 removal grounds (specifics UNKNOWN)
- What "stopped" means: the affected member does not participate in the specific decision (self-disqualification) or is removed from the Board (member removal)
- Independence justification: §1309.05 is self-executing; §1309.02 removal authority is held by the appointing authority outside the Board itself
- Post-stop review process and timeline: case-by-case for self-disqualification; member removal per ordinance procedure

ANNOTATION: STOP PATH 3 is a narrow internal mechanism (per-decision and per-member) rather than a full-Board stop authority. The framework typically expects stop paths that can halt the entire authority's operation; counting self-disqualification as a stop path is generous but reflects the structural reality that ABR review is decision-by-decision and a recused or removed member is genuinely outside that decision.

**5.4 — Penalty for activating stop:**
- [x] Confirmed — no formal penalty for filing appeals or pursuing judicial review per ORC §2506. Applicants who appeal may face delay costs but no procedural penalty.

**5.5 — Stop-path independence verification:**
- [x] Yes — courts can act without ABR cooperation. Council can act without Board cooperation. Self-disqualification and removal are internal but structurally independent of the affected decision.

ANNOTATION: the three declared stop paths are weaker than typical municipal authority stop-path declarations. STOP PATH 1 (judicial appeal) is the only fully external mechanism, and it operates after the Board's decision has been issued. There is no equivalent of a citizen review board or independent oversight body specific to ABR. Stakeholders may challenge this stop-path structure under SCBP-08.

**6.4 — Capture risk identification:**
- [x] Yes
  - *Member-pool capture vector.* Per §1309.03, all three members must be Ohio-licensed architects who are also Shaker Heights residents. This is a narrow pool. The Board judges design decisions affecting property values in a city where the members are themselves property owners and where their professional practice may overlap with applicants. §1309.05 (self-interest disqualification) addresses individual conflicts; structural concentration in the architect-resident pool is a population-level capture vector not addressable by per-decision disqualification alone.
  - *Architectural-profession alignment.* Members are by definition architectural professionals; design standards developed by such professionals may reflect professional aesthetic preferences that diverge from owner preferences. The framework treats this as a structural disclosure under R03 (Institutional Capture Prohibition).
  - *Real-estate-market dependency.* ABR design standards are publicly framed as preserving property values. This creates structural alignment between ABR decisions and real-estate-market preferences, which in turn correlate with demographic patterns. Per R55/R59, ABR and the broader Shaker Heights real-estate-value-preservation system function as a coordinated authority.
  - *Funding concentration:* the ABR is funded 100% by the City of Shaker Heights through Planning Department allocation. The 33% concentration threshold is exceeded by definition.
