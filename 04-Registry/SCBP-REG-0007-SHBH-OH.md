# Mechanism Record — Shaker Heights Department of Building & Housing

## SECTION 1 — What is this authority?

**1.1 — Mechanism name:** Shaker Heights Department of Building & Housing (SHBH), sometimes referred to as the Building Department or Building & Housing Department.

**1.2 — Functional description:**
The authority is the municipal building, housing, and property-maintenance enforcement department for the City of Shaker Heights, Ohio. It reviews construction plans and issues residential and commercial building permits; inspects new construction, alterations, and existing properties for compliance with the Ohio Building Code, the Shaker Heights Codified Ordinances Building Code (Part Thirteen), the Housing Code (Part Fourteen), and related provisions. The authority issues licenses for contractors, exterior painters, landscapers, and rental housing. The authority conducts Point of Sale (POS) inspections required before any residential transfer of title, issues Certificates of Inspection and Certificates of Compliance, and administers POS escrow accounts holding funds equal to 150% of estimated repair costs. The authority issues Notices of Violation, Stop Work Orders, and Orders to Abate, may revoke permits, and refers cases for prosecution as first-degree misdemeanors (up to 6 months in jail and/or $1,000 fine per Chapter 1415 violations). The authority manages the city's Land Bank program for vacant lots and partners with the Fire Department on apartment-building POS inspections.

## SECTION 5 — What stops it?

**STOP PATH 1 — Shaker Heights Board of Appeals / Board of Building Code Appeals**
- Who can activate it: any property owner, contractor, seller, buyer, or affected party receiving a Notice of Violation, Stop Work Order, or escrow determination
- Trigger condition: filing of written appeal within 30 days of notice; Board of Building Code Appeals operates per Chapter 1311 of the Codified Ordinances; Board of Appeals operates per Chapter 132
- What "stopped" means: the Board may grant, deny, grant in part, or deny in part the appeal; may grant time extensions; may issue variances from escrow requirements (per §1415.05(g))
- Independence justification: the Boards are established by ordinance with members appointed by the Mayor and confirmed by Council; the Director of Law (or designee) serves as Secretary; the Boards operate outside the department's chain of command. ANNOTATION: independence is municipal rather than fully external — appointments are by the Mayor, who oversees the department director. Substantive independence is a question for stakeholder review under R32.
- Post-stop review process and timeline: hearings are recorded; decision issued within 14 days after hearing; the Board of Building Code Appeals submits an annual report to the Mayor and Council per §1311.06.

**STOP PATH 2 — Ohio Courts and Ohio Board of Building Standards (OBBS)**
- Who can activate it: any party with standing; OBBS may audit and revoke departmental certifications
- Trigger condition: judicial proceeding (declaratory judgment, mandamus, civil action under ORC §3781.19 and §3791.04); OBBS administrative review
- What "stopped" means: judicial invalidation of departmental action; OBBS revocation of residential or commercial building code certification (which would require the city to contract out enforcement)
- Independence justification: state courts and OBBS operate outside municipal authority
- Post-stop review process and timeline: judicial timelines vary; OBBS processes are administrative

**STOP PATH 3 — Shaker Heights City Council**
- Who can activate it: Shaker Heights City Council, by ordinance amending the Building Code, Housing Code, POS provisions, or department budget; or by resolution directing departmental practice
- Trigger condition: judgment-based, expressed through legislative action. The May 2025 POS amendments demonstrate exercise of this authority.
- What "stopped" means: Council can amend code provisions, defund operations, restructure authority, or (in extremis) abolish the department through Charter action
- Independence justification: Council is elected by Shaker Heights voters and operates outside the department's chain of command
- Post-stop review process and timeline: legislative process on Council's calendar

**5.4 — Penalty for activating stop:**
- [x] Confirmed — no formal penalty applies for filing appeals or pursuing judicial review. Appellants may present evidence, be represented by counsel, and examine witnesses (per §1301.08(d)).

**5.5 — Stop-path independence verification:**
