# Mechanism Record — Shaker Heights Police Department

## SECTION 1 — What is this authority?

**1.1 — Mechanism name:** Shaker Heights Police Department (SHPD)

**1.2 — Functional description:**
The authority is the municipal police department for the City of Shaker Heights, Ohio. It conducts patrol operations, responds to emergency and non-emergency calls for service, conducts criminal investigations, makes arrests under Ohio Revised Code authority, conducts traffic stops and issues citations, executes search warrants, and operates a records and evidence function. The authority may use force, including deadly force, under Ohio law and departmental policy. The authority may detain persons, conduct custodial interrogations, and refer cases for prosecution. The authority operates a Crisis Intervention component for responses involving mental health concerns and partners with the City's social worker program for non-emergency mental health calls.

## SECTION 5 — What stops it?

**STOP PATH 1 — Shaker Heights City Council**
- Who can activate it: Shaker Heights City Council, by ordinance or resolution. The Council holds budget appropriation authority over the department.
- Trigger condition: judgment-based, expressed through Council legislative action
- What "stopped" means: Council can defund operations, restructure authority, impose policy directives via ordinance, or (in extremis) abolish the department through Charter action. The 2023 "Framework for a Safe and Just Shaker" resolution is an example of Council-directed operational change.
- Independence justification: Council is elected by Shaker Heights voters and operates outside the department's chain of command. The Mayor (separately elected) is also outside the department's chain of command.
- Post-stop review process and timeline: legislative process operates on Council's calendar; emergency action available under home-rule authority.

**STOP PATH 2 — Cuyahoga County Courts and Ohio Courts (judicial check)**
- Who can activate it: any party with standing in state or federal court, including criminal defendants, civil-rights plaintiffs, and the Cuyahoga County Prosecutor declining prosecution
- Trigger condition: both — threshold-based (constitutional violations) and judgment-based (judicial review of specific actions)
- What "stopped" means: suppression of evidence, civil judgments, injunctive relief, criminal prosecution of officers in extreme cases. Conviction declines materially affect operational pattern.
- Independence justification: state and federal courts are structurally independent of the executive branch and of municipal authority.
- Post-stop review process and timeline: judicial timelines vary; civil-rights litigation under 42 U.S.C. §1983 is the standard external check.

**STOP PATH 3 — State and Federal Oversight**
- Who can activate it: Ohio Attorney General (training and certification revocation via Ohio Peace Officer Training Commission), U.S. Department of Justice Civil Rights Division (pattern-or-practice investigations), CALEA accreditation revocation (loss of accreditation)
- Trigger condition: threshold-based — accreditation standards, statutory authority, and federal civil-rights statutes
- What "stopped" means: individual officer decertification, departmental consent decree or DOJ oversight, loss of CALEA accreditation
- Independence justification: state-level peace officer certification operates outside municipal authority; federal civil-rights enforcement is independent of state and local authority; CALEA is an independent professional accreditation body.
- Post-stop review process and timeline: variable; DOJ pattern-or-practice investigations typically take 1–3 years if initiated.

ANNOTATION: The 2023 ballot campaign sought to create a Stop Path 4 — an independent civilian oversight board with binding authority. The Council-adopted Framework compromise reportedly included community-driven elements but, based on public sources reviewed, did not create a fully independent civilian board with binding stop authority. The compromise's substantive independence relative to R32 (Independent Stop Authority) is a finding for stakeholder review.

**5.4 — Penalty for activating stop:**
- [x] Confirmed — no formal penalty applies. Civil-rights plaintiffs, complainants, and citizens invoking Council or court processes are not formally penalized.
- ANNOTATION: informal penalties (retaliation risk, social or professional cost) are documented in U.S. policing literature generally and are not specifically addressable from Shaker Heights public sources.
