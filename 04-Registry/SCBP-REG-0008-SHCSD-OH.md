# Mechanism Record — Shaker Heights City School District

## SECTION 1 — What is this authority?

**1.1 — Mechanism name:** Shaker Heights City School District (SHCSD), referred to publicly as Shaker Heights Schools or Shaker Schools.

**1.2 — Functional description:**
The authority is a public school district operating eight schools serving approximately 4,445 students in grades PK–12 across the City of Shaker Heights and an approximately 1-square-mile area of the City of Cleveland surrounding Shaker Square. The district operates five K–4 elementary schools (Boulevard, Fernway, Lomond, Mercer, Onaway), Woodbury for grades 5–6, Shaker Heights Middle School for grades 7–8, Shaker Heights High School for grades 9–12, and the Ludlow Early Learning Center for PK (opening Fall 2025). The district issues binding determinations through compulsory-attendance enforcement under Ohio Revised Code §3321, student discipline (detention, in-school suspension, out-of-school suspension of 1–10 days, emergency removal, expulsion of 20–80 days), assessment-based grade and graduation determinations, special-education placement and IEP determinations under IDEA and Ohio law, Title IX and harassment investigations, transcript and credential issuance, residency verification and enrollment determinations, and employment decisions for approximately 430 teachers and additional staff. The district holds taxing authority through voter-approved property tax levies under HB 920, and authorizes bond issuance for capital projects subject to voter approval. The high school operates a full International Baccalaureate (IB) program.

## SECTION 5 — What stops it?

**STOP PATH 1 — Voters and Board of Education**
- Who can activate it: registered voters within the district through Board of Education elections; through levy and bond ballot measures
- Trigger condition: judgment-based, expressed through electoral action. Levy and bond measures require voter approval (the failed-and-renewed November 2025 levy demonstrates active exercise of this authority). Board members are elected.
- What "stopped" means: voters can defeat funding measures (constraining operations), elect new Board members, or in extremis vote against district continuation (though district structure under Ohio law makes outright dissolution rare)
- Independence justification: voters and Board are elected; Board operates outside administrative chain of command
- Post-stop review process and timeline: regular Board meetings; Treasurer's Five-Year Forecast cycle; levy cycles on Ohio election calendar

ANNOTATION: while structurally independent, the Board hires and oversees the Superintendent, who is the chief operating officer. The Board's separation from district operations is real but not absolute; Board members are politically connected to the district they oversee.

**STOP PATH 2 — Courts and Federal Agencies**
- Who can activate it: students, parents, employees, and third parties through state and federal courts; U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights (OCR); state administrative due-process hearings under IDEA
- Trigger condition: both — threshold-based (constitutional and statutory violations) and judgment-based (judicial review)
- What "stopped" means: judicial invalidation of specific actions; IDEA due-process determinations binding the district; OCR findings and resolution agreements; civil judgments under 42 U.S.C. §1983, Title IX, Title VI, IDEA, Section 504, ADA
- Independence justification: state and federal courts and OCR are structurally independent of the district
- Post-stop review process and timeline: judicial timelines vary; IDEA due-process hearings on statutory timeline; OCR investigations typically multi-year

**STOP PATH 3 — Ohio Department of Education and Workforce / State Oversight**
- Who can activate it: Ohio Department of Education and Workforce (ODEW); state auditor; ODE in cases of fiscal caution, fiscal watch, or fiscal emergency
- Trigger condition: threshold-based — academic accountability standards, fiscal health standards, statutory compliance
- What "stopped" means: state oversight including loss of local control under fiscal emergency; report-card determinations; state-imposed academic distress commission for distressed districts; licensure-revocation for individual personnel by State Board of Education
- Independence justification: state authority operates outside municipal and district structure
- Post-stop review process and timeline: per Ohio statute; fiscal watch and emergency processes are multi-year

ANNOTATION: the April 7, 2025 Board meeting summary explicitly identified state oversight risk: "The district could face state oversight or loss of local control due to fiscal caution or emergency status" if the levy were not passed. This is the most active Stop Path 3 risk currently documented.

**5.4 — Penalty for activating stop:**
- [x] Confirmed — no formal penalty for filing complaints, due-process requests, ballot opposition, OCR complaints, or judicial action. Retaliation against employees for protected activity is prohibited under Title VII, IDEA, Title IX, and Ohio law.
